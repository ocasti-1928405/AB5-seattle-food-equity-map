About This Project

A critical examination of food access through the lens of affordability

Project Overview

The Seattle Food Equity Map is an interactive web application that challenges conventional food desert mapping by incorporating economic accessibility alongside geographic proximity. While traditional approaches like the USDA Food Access Research Atlas identify areas with limited supermarket access, our project reveals a more nuanced reality: many neighborhoods have stores nearby, but lack access to affordable options.

By classifying grocery stores into three price tiers‚ÄîBudget ($), Mid-Range ($$), and Premium ($$$)‚Äîwe provide policymakers, community organizations, and residents with actionable insights about where affordable food options are truly lacking.

Our Approach

1. Data Collection

We compiled data from multiple authoritative sources: the USDA Food Access Research Atlas (2019), Seattle Active Business License database, US Census TIGER/Line shapefiles, and Seattle's emergency food provider listings.

2. Manual Classification

Each grocery store and convenience store was manually classified into price tiers based on market positioning, typical product prices, and brand recognition. This crucial step adds the "economic access" layer missing from standard food desert maps.

3. Interactive Visualization

Built with Mapbox GL JS, our map allows users to filter stores by price tier, view emergency food services, calculate distances from their location, and explore neighborhood-level food access patterns.

4. Critical Analysis

By overlaying income data from the American Community Survey with our price-adjusted food access layer, we reveal disparities that standard proximity-only models miss.
